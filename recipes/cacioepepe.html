Cacio e Pepe

(The recipe was taken from Bon appetit)

Description

Cacio e pepe is one of Rome's essential pasta dishes, served at restaurants all over the Italian capital. Like many of the Eternal City's best dishes, a good cacio e pepe recipe is about simplicity. Classic versions call for just pasta, salted water, freshly ground pepper, and Pecorino Romano, a Roman sheep's milk cheese.

Ours tosses a bit of Grana Padano or Parmesan into the mix for a more rounded cheese flavor that melds warmth, nuttiness, and sharpness.

Ingredients

  • Pasta (such as egg tagliolini, bucatini, or spaghetti): 170 gr
  • Unsalted butter(cudeb, divided): 45 gr
  • Coarsely ground black pepper, plus more: 15 gr
  • Finely grated Grana Padano or Parmesan cheese: 200 gr
  • Finely grated Pecorino Romano cheese: 80 gr
  • Salt: to taste

Steps of preparation

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add your pasta and cook, stirring occasionally, until about 2 minutes before tender. Drain, reserving 200 ml of pasta cooking water.
  2. Meanwhile, melt the butter, add coarsely ground black pepper and cook, swirling pan, until toasted about 1 min.
  3. Add half of the reserved pasta water to the butter and bring to a simmer. Add cooked pasta. Reduce burner to low heat and add Grana Padano and Parmesan, stirring and tossing with tongs until melted. Remove pan from heat; add Pecorino Romano, stirring and tossing until cheese melts and sauce coats the pasta (Add more pasta water if sauce seems dry).
  4. Transfer pasta to warm bowls, garnish with more black pepper, and serve.
